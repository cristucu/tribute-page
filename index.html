Henric VIII and the Tudors

The king with 6 wives.

    Here's a time line of Henry VIII of England:

  • 1491 - born at Palace of Placentia in Greenwich, Kent, the third child of the first Tudor monarch, Henry VII, and his wife, Elizabeth Plantagenet, daughter of the Yorkist king, Edward IV.
  • 1502 - his brother, Arthur dies(possible of sweating sickness).
  • 1509 - Henry's father, Henry VII dies of tuberculosis - Henry VIII becomes king of England at just 17 years.
  • 1509 - he marries Catherine of Aragon, his brother's widow - princess of Spain.
  • 1511 - first son is born, Henry - dies after 7 weeks.
  • 1513 - invades France.
  • 1516 - Mary is born.
  • 1519 - King's mistress, Elizabeth Blount gives birth to an illegitimate son - Henry Fitzroy.
  • 1533 - annulment of his marriage with Catherine of Aragon. The king believed that God was punishing him for marrying his brother's widow and that is the reason for not having a living son. - he marries Anne Boleyn
  • 1533 - another daughter is born - Elizabeth
  • 1536 - beheading of Anne Boleyn. One day later,Henry gets engaged to Jane Syemore, one of the Queen's ladies-in-wating. They were married 10 days later.
  • 1537 - Queen Jane gave birth to a son - Prince Edward, the future Eduard VI. The birth was difficult and the queen died two weeks later of infection.
  • 1540 - in order to keep the realm safe against a possible Roman Church attack, the king was proposed Anne of Cleves the 25 years old sister of Duke of Cleves, who was considered an important ally. The king liked the portrait painted by Hans Holbein the Young and agreed to marry her, but he soon looked for reasons to get the marriage anulled. Queen Anne agreed and declared the marriage haven't been consumated. He marries the fifth wife, Catherine Howard, a 17 years old girl.
  • 1542 - Queen Catherine Howards is beheaded, due to adultery charges. She had an affair with courtier Thomas Culpepper and also employed Francis Dereham,who had previously been informally engaged to her and had an affair with her prior to her marriage, as her secretary.
  • 1543 - he marries Catherine Parra wealthy widow.
  • 1547 - King Henry VIII of Englanddies in the Pallace of Whitehall. Upon his death, he was succeeded by his son, Edward VI.
Two beheadings out of six wives is too many. - Henry VIII of England.
